# Product Hunt Pricing Psychology

This skill helps you leverage psychological principles to make your Product Hunt offers more compelling and drive higher conversions.

## When to Use This Skill

- Structuring your launch offer
- Creating compelling pricing displays
- Building urgency and scarcity
- Maximizing perceived value
- Optimizing conversion messaging

## Core Psychological Principles

### 1. Anchoring Effect

**Principle:** First number seen influences perception of all following numbers.

**Application:**
```
‚ùå Weak: "$99/year"
‚úì Strong: "$299/year ‚Üí $99/year (67% off!)"
```

**How to Use:**
- Always show original price first
- Make the discount visible
- Calculate and show savings

**Example:**
```
Regular: $49/month ($588/year)
Product Hunt: $19/month ($228/year)

You save: $360/year
```

---

### 2. Loss Aversion

**Principle:** People fear losing more than they enjoy gaining.

**Application:**
```
‚ùå Weak: "Get 30% off"
‚úì Strong: "Don't miss 30% off ‚Äî ends in 24 hours"
```

**How to Use:**
- Frame as avoiding loss, not just gaining
- Emphasize what they'll miss
- Create fear of missing out

**Examples:**
- "Lock in this price before it's gone"
- "Only 23 spots remaining"
- "This offer won't come back"

---

### 3. Scarcity Principle

**Principle:** Limited availability increases perceived value.

**Types of Scarcity:**

**Quantity Scarcity:**
```
"Limited to 100 lifetime licenses"
"Only 15 spots left"
"First 50 users only"
```

**Time Scarcity:**
```
"48 hours only"
"Ends Sunday midnight"
"Launch week special"
```

**Combination:**
```
"First 100 users OR until Sunday ‚Äî
whichever comes first"
```

### 4. Social Proof

**Principle:** People follow what others do.

**Application in Pricing:**
```
‚úì "Join 500+ users who chose the Pro plan"
‚úì "Most popular" badge on middle tier
‚úì "12 people bought this in the last hour"
```

**How to Use:**
- Show which plan is most chosen
- Display recent activity
- Include testimonials near pricing

### 5. Price Framing

**Principle:** How price is presented affects perception.

**Per-Day Framing:**
```

**Comparison Framing:**
```

**Savings Framing:**

**Principle:** Adding a less attractive option makes target option look better.

**How It Works:**

### 7. Zero Price Effect

**Principle:** "Free" has irrational appeal beyond its actual value.

**Application:**
```
‚úì "Start free, upgrade when ready"
‚úì "Free forever for personal use"
‚úì "No credit card required"
```

**Freemium Psychology:**
- Lower barrier to try
- Build habit before payment
- Reduce perceived risk

---

### 8. Charm Pricing

**Principle:** Prices ending in 9 or 7 feel significantly lower.

**Application:**
```
‚ùå $100 (feels like "a hundred")
‚úì $99 (feels like "ninety-something")
‚úì $97 (feels even cheaper)
```

**When to Use:**
- Consumer products: $X.99
- Professional/SaaS: $X9 or $X7
- Premium/luxury: Round numbers ($100, $500)

---

### 9. Bundle Psychology

**Principle:** Bundling obscures individual item values.

**Application:**
```
INDIVIDUAL:
- Product A: $49
- Product B: $49
- Product C: $49
Total: $147

BUNDLE:
All three for $99 (Save $48!)
```

**Why It Works:**
- Hard to evaluate bundle fairly
- Focus shifts to total savings
- Perceived value increases

## Pricing Page Best Practices

### Visual Hierarchy
- Highlight recommended plan
- Use "Most Popular" badges
- Make preferred option visually dominant

### Feature Comparison
- Show what's included at each tier
- Use checkmarks liberally
- Dim features not included

### Trust Elements
- Money-back guarantee
- Testimonials near pricing
- Security badges
- Social proof numbers

### Call-to-Action
- Action-oriented buttons ("Start Free", "Get Access")
- Reduce friction ("No credit card required")
- Reinforce value ("Get started in 60 seconds")
